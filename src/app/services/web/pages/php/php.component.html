Understanding Requirements

Get in touch with our client

The first step in proposing a solution is to establish contact with the client. This may involve reaching out through email, phone, or a meeting to discuss their needs and understand the problem they are facing.
Understand client's needs

The next step is to understand the client's needs and requirements. This involves active listening, asking the right questions, and clarifying any doubts. It is important to have a clear understanding of the problem the client is facing before proposing a solution.
Suggest solution

Based on the client's needs and requirements, the proposed solution is suggested. The solution may include various options, each with its advantages and disadvantages. The solution proposed should be practical, cost-effective, and tailored to the client's needs.

Finalizing solution

After the proposed solution is presented, the client is given an opportunity to provide feedback and ask any questions. Any concerns or questions raised are addressed, and the solution is refined until a final solution is agreed upon. Once the solution is finalized, the project is planned and executed to meet the client's needs.

UX & Design

Start designing

After the structure is in place, the UX and Design team begins designing the website or application. This includes creating wireframes and prototypes to determine the layout, structure, and functionality of the user interface.
Design each page

Once the wireframes and prototypes are approved, the UX and Design team begins designing each page of the website or application. This includes creating a visual design, selecting fonts, colors, and images, and ensuring that the design is consistent with the brand's identity.

Development

Start developing

The first step in Development is to start building the website or application. This involves writing code, setting up the development environment, and ensuring that all necessary tools and libraries are installed.
Develop main functions

After the initial setup is complete, the Development team begins developing the main functions of the website or application. This includes creating features, implementing business logic, and integrating with external systems if required.
Fixing bugs

Once the main functions are implemented, the Development team tests the website or application to identify any issues or bugs that may impact the user experience. Any bugs or issues are fixed to ensure that the website or application functions as expected.

Testing

Start testing

The first step in Testing is to start testing the website or application. This involves conducting various types of testing such as functional testing, performance testing, security testing, and usability testing.
Client reviewed

Once the testing is complete, the client is given an opportunity to review and provide feedback. Any issues or bugs identified by the client are fixed, and the website or application is tested again to ensure that it meets the client's requirements.
Final review

After addressing the client's feedback, a final review of the website or application is conducted to ensure that it is ready for deployment.

Deployment

Time to deploy

The first step in Deployment is to prepare the website or application for deployment. This includes preparing the server, configuring the database, and optimizing the code.
Deploy to client's server

Once the website or application is ready, it is deployed to the client's server. This may involve working with the client's IT team to ensure that the deployment is successful.
Ready-to-use

After the deployment is complete, the website or application is ready-to-use.

Support & Maintenance

Support Team

After the website or application is deployed, a support team is assigned to provide ongoing support to the client. This may include addressing any issues or bugs that arise, providing updates, and answering any questions the client may have.
Hand-over to client

Once the support team is in place, the website or application is handed over to the client. The client is given access to the code and any necessary documentation.
Maintenance

The support team continues to provide ongoing maintenance to the website or application. This may involve fixing bugs, addressing security concerns, and providing updates as necessary to ensure that the website or application continues to function as intended.
